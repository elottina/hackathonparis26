Judging Rubric
36-hour hackathon · June 27–28, 2026 · In partnership with Y Combinator
Every team is scored out of 100 on the six universal criteria below. Teams also receive up to 15 additional points on
the one track-specific criterion matching their track, for a maximum of 115. Judge by proof, not demo vibes.
PART 1 — UNIVERSAL CRITERIA (100 PTS)
1. Problem Clarity 0–17 pts
Key Question: Is the pain real? Can they articulate who has it and why?
SCORE CRITERIA
15–17 Crystal clear problem, specific target user, urgent pain point with evidence
10–14 Clear problem, defined user segment, believable pain point
5–9 Somewhat vague problem, generic user, unclear urgency
0–4 Unclear or non-existent problem, no defined user
2. Demo Quality 0–17 pts
Key Question: Does it work? Does it show the core value prop clearly?
SCORE CRITERIA
15–17 Fully functional demo, clearly demonstrates value, polished UX
10–14 Working demo with minor issues, value prop visible
5–9 Partially working, value prop unclear or buried
0–4 Non-functional or missing demo
3. Pricing & Business Model Key Question: Who pays? How much? Do the unit economics work?
0–17 pts
SCORE CRITERIA
15–17 Clear monetization, realistic pricing, sound unit economics
10–14 Defined revenue model, reasonable pricing assumptions
5–9 Vague monetization, questionable economics
0–4 No clear path to revenue

4. Go-To-Market Strategy 0–17 pts
Key Question: How do you reach customers? What's the first $10K plan?
SCORE CRITERIA
15–17 Specific channels, realistic customer acquisition plan, clear first steps
10–14 Defined channels, reasonable acquisition strategy
5–9 Generic marketing ideas, unclear path to first customers
0–4 No go-to-market strategy
5. Differentiation 0–16 pts
Key Question: Why you vs. alternatives? What's your moat?
SCORE CRITERIA
14–16 Clear competitive advantage, defensible moat, unique insight
9–13 Some differentiation, aware of competition
4–8 Minimal differentiation, unclear competitive landscape
0–3 No differentiation or awareness of alternatives
6. Pitch Delivery 0–16 pts
Key Question: Clear, compelling, confident storytelling?
SCORE CRITERIA
14–16 Engaging, confident, clear narrative arc, great time management
9–13 Good presentation, mostly clear, decent flow
4–8 Unclear structure, lacking confidence, timing issues
0–3 Poor delivery, confusing, significantly over/under time

7. Agentic Depth 0–15 pts
SOFTWARE FOR AGENTS • TRACK-SPECIFIC
Key Question: Is this real agentic engineering, or a prompt wrapped in a UI?
SCORE CRITERIA
13–15 Genuine multi-step autonomy, robust tool use, recovers from failure/edge cases, defensible
beyond the base model
9–12 Working agent loop with some reliability, light tool use, modest moat
5–8 Mostly scripted or single-step, thin wrapper, brittle under any deviation
0–4 Prompt-in-a-box, no real agentic behavior
7. Physical Execution 0–15 pts
ROBOTICS & HARDWARE • TRACK-SPECIFIC
Key Question: Does the hardware actually work, live, in the room?
SCORE CRITERIA
13–15 Runs live with real sensing / actuation, non-trivial integration overcome in 36h
9–12 Works live with hiccups, real hardware, some integration depth
5–8 Partial or unreliable, leans on video, shallow integration
0–4 No working hardware; simulation or video presented as proof
7. Ambition & Why-Now 0–15 pts
OPEN TRACK • TRACK-SPECIFIC
Key Question: Is this a bold, well-timed bet, or a generic AI wrapper?
SCORE CRITERIA
13–15 Bold, non-obvious bet with a sharp why-now, plus a real working core
9–12 Solid idea, reasonable timing, functional build
5–8 Incremental or derivative, weak why-now
0–4 Generic AI wrapper, no insight or timing rationale

⏰ Submission Deadline
All projects must be submitted through the submission form before 1:00 PM Paris time on Sunday, June 28.

Your submission must include:

A public GitHub repository containing the full codebase
A clear README.md explaining:

  
What your project does
How it works
How to install and run it
A demo video
A pitch deck

🎥 Demo Video
Your video must be 5 minutes maximum.

The format is completely open, so feel free to be creative. You can make:

A YC-style product demo
An advertisement
A traditional pitch video
Anything else that clearly presents your project

There is a big bonus if you manage to wow the judges. ✨

⚖️ Judging Process
Between 1:00 PM and 4:00 PM, every submission video will be reviewed and scored by three different judges.

The judging criteria is detailled in the PDF file. 

The scores from the three judges will be aggregated. At 4:00 PM, we will announce:

The best projects from each track
The global Top 5 projects

The Top 5 teams will move on to a second round at 5:00 PM, where they will deliver a live pitch in front of a YC jury.
