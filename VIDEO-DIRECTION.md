## 1. CREATIVE DIRECTION

### The big idea
A true-crime documentary about a crime with a **perfect victim, a flawless alibi, and no body in the transcript** — until a detective who watches the hands instead of the mouth closes the case live, on a real AI agent, in 90 seconds.

- The **agent is the suspect.** The **transcript is the alibi.** **Rogue is the detective.**
- The whole film is a **case file being opened (0:14) and closed (4:50).** That frame lets us be gripping in Act 1 and ruthlessly clear in Act 2 — every business beat lands as "evidence," not a feature list.
- **The crime and the demo climax on the SAME frame:** the cold open's three red exfil pings (0:14) are paid off by the real product's hero breach card (2:55). That recognition *is* the WOW.

### Tone
Restrained true-crime narrator. Never raised. Slow. Cello, low piano. **Confidence, not hype.** The restraint is itself the credibility move the mentors rewarded ("brutal honesty / no overclaiming"). Emotional arc: **dread → comprehension → relief → control.** We make the jury feel the helplessness of a small HR-tech CTO who would *never* catch this — then hand them the detective. We end on **control, not fear.**

### Why it beats 100 generic AI-wrapper demos
Everyone else opens on a logo, a problem statement, and a dashboard. We open on a **victim and a crime** — emotion first, product second — and our single best asset (the literal "said vs did" card) is *also* the narrative payoff. The true-crime frame turns dry compliance (EU AI Act, GDPR) into stakes a YC jury *feels*. No other team can run this concept, because no other team has a **behavior oracle that can actually solve the crime on camera.**

### The one risk — and the rule that kills it
**Risk:** over-styling until the cold open reads as fiction/an ad, which would undercut "judge by proof."
**Rule:** the screen-recording must be **visibly the real product, unstaged, with real timestamps and the real listener catching real bytes.** Style sells the stakes; the product closes the case. **We keep one raw, unstyled stretch of live UI (2:30) so the styling never reads as concealment. If any beat looks faked, cut the style — never the proof.**

### Integrity note carried into production (read this before shooting the demo)
The video shows **two real, separately-labeled captures of the same target**, never one run cut to look like more:
1. **Depth / swarm run** = paste the real TalentScreen URL → **Run →** (preset `fast`: multi-vector, 4 turns). This is the adaptive manhunt — most doors hold, it changes tactic, one opens.
2. **Black-box hero run** = click **"Scan a live HR screening agent"** (preset `hr`: TalentScreen exposes zero tools, leaks the candidate's name on the wire). This produces the hero breach card.
Narration names them as two passes on the same agent. We never imply the swarm reel and the name-card came from one click.
